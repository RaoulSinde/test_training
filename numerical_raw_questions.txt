Which country had the highest ratio of Motorway-kilometers-per-person in 2010?

Bulgaria

Germany

Italy

Spain ##Bonne réponse##

Explanation
Data Interpretation
From the table & chart we need:

Total population in 2010 for all 5 countries

Total length of Motorways in 2010 for all 5
countries
From the question text we need:

Formula of the ratio (Motorway-kilometers-per-
person)
Reasoning
We must evaluate the Motorway-kilometers-per-
person ratios for all five countries and compare them
to find the country with the highest ratio.

Calculation
It is important to note that the population data has
been provided in millions and the Motorways lengths
are given in thousands of kilometers. But since we
only need to compare the ratios and not evaluate the
exact values of the ratios, it is alright to use the data
as is without conversion to units.

Motorway-kilometers-per-person ratio = Total
Motorways length / Total population

Bulgaria: 0.44 / 7.42 ~ 0.059

Germany: 12.82 / 81.80 ~ 0.157

Italy: 6.67 / 59.19 ~ 0.113

Netherlands: 2.65 / 16.57 ~ 0.160

Spain: 14.26 / 46.49 ~ 0.307

Spain has the highest Motorway-kilometers-per-
person ratio amongst the five countries by a fair
margin.

Potential Pitfalls and Shortcuts
We do not need to calculate the exact Motorway-
kilometers-per-person ratios; we only need to find a
relative value for each country which is quick and easy
to calculate. In this case, we can use the population
data (in millions) and the Motorway length data (in
thousands km) directly. If we were to incorporate the
zeroes for the millions and the zeroes for the
thousands in our calculation, not only would it take
more time it would also make our calculations prone
to mistakes.

It is important to read the data for 2010 only and not
to accidentally read the adjacent data for other years.
Additionally, we must be careful not to divide the
motorway length in one country with the population
of another.

We know that the Motorway length should be in the
numerator of the ratio and the population figure
should be in the denominator. At a glance, we can
approximate the ratios of each country (without even
making exact calculations). For example, in Bulgaria's
case the numerator is roughly 0.5 and the
denominator is roughly 8, therefore the ratio is in the
range of 1 : 16. Similarly, the ratio of Spain is roughly
15: 45 which simplifies to 1 : 3. This (much quicker)
method also reveals that the country with the highest
ratio is Spain.

Assuming that males and females were born in equal proportions, which of these 3 countries showed the least improvement in life expectancy from 2018 to 2023?

Czechia

Czechia & Romania

Romania

Switzerland ##Bonne réponse##

Romania & Switzerland

Explanation
Data Interpretation
From the provided table, we will be using all the data
values for life expectancy at birth in the 3 noted
countries for 2 different years. From the question text,
we will use the info that males and females can be
assumed to have been born in equal proportions.

Reasoning
Since we are assuming that males and females were
born in equal proportions, the overall life expectancy
for a country in a given year is the simple average of
the male and female life expectancy. Once, we have
calculated the life expectancy of each given country
for 2018 and 2023, we can find the difference to
determine which country showed the least
improvement.

Calculation
Life expectancy at birth (in years):
Czechia (2018): (76.2 + 82.0) / 2 = 79.1
Czechia (2023): (76.9 + 82.9) / 2 = 79.9
Czechia (difference): 79.9 - 79.1 = +0.8

Romania (2018): (71.7 + 79.2) / 2 = 75.45
Romania (2023): (72.6 + 80.4) / 2 = 76.5
Romania (difference): 76.5 - 75.45 = +1.05

Switzerland (2018): (81.9 +85.7) / 2 = 83.8
Switzerland (2023): (82.4 +86.0) / 2 = 84.2
Switzerland (difference): 84.2 - 83.8 = +0.4

Among the 3 countries, Switzerland showed the least
improvement in life expectancy at birth between 2018
and 2023.

Potential Shortcuts / Pitfalls
It is critical not to average out the male life expectancy
and the female life expectancy for different years. The
overall life expectancy is the average of the male and
female life expectancy for the same year.

The question asked for the least improvement in life
expectancy, not the biggest improvement. Be careful.

Since the life expectancy in Switzerland is already very
high, it is hard to improve too much on it.

Calculation

(X* (100% + 39.8%)) * (100% + 7.8%)

The dollar value of almond milk sold in 2015, in terms
of X, can be found by multiplying this quantity by the
liter price (i.e. $2 per liter):
((X* (100% + 39.8%)) * (100% + 7.8%)) * $2

We already know from the chart that the exact dollar
value of almond milk sold in 2015 was $894,650,036.
Therefore by equating the above-noted expression
with the dollar value, we get:
((X*(100% + 39.8%)) * (100% + 7.8%)) * $2 = $894 650
036
((X*(139.8%)) * (107.8%)) * $2 = $894 650 036
((X*(139.8 / 100)) * (107.8/ 100)) * $2 = $894 650
036
(X* 1.398 * 1.078) * $2 = $894 650 036
(X* 1.507044) * $2 = $894 650 036
X*$3.014088 = $894 650 036
X =$894 650 036 / $3.014088
X = 296 822 799 liters
X = 297 million liters

Potential Pitfalls and Shortcuts
We have taken the quantity of almond milk sold in

To calculate which of the potential answer options is
the period with the smallest percentage increase in
average price of coastal property in Croatia we

proportion. If we then multiply this by 100 our figures

Explanation

Data Interpretation

From the table provided, we will be using the data
values referring to the population of Romania along
with the percentage of the population making up the
workforce and the percentage of the workforce that is
unemployed in 2000 and 2005 only. All other data
provided by the table are irrelevant to our question.
From the question text itself, we will be using the
information regarding the increase in the population
of Romania between 2000 and 2005.

Reasoning

To calculate the number of unemployed people we
need to multiply the percentage of the workforce that
is unemployed by the size of the workforce and to
calculate the size of the workforce we need to
multiply the percentage of the population that makes
up the workforce by the size of the population. We can
then calculate the difference.

Calculation

Firstly we need to calculate the size of the workforce
in 2005:

21635 *0.408 = 8827

The number unemployed in 2005 is then:

8827 *0.091 = 803.26

Before we can do the same for 2000 we need to
calculate the population in 2000 which was:

21635 / 1.018 = 21252

The number unemployed in 2000 was:

21252 * 0.396 * 0.092 = 774.25

This is difference of:

803.26 - 774.25 = 29

The population is given in thousands and so the
answer is also in thousands.

Potential Shortcuts / Pitfalls

Don't forget to adjust the population for the 2000
calculation. Note that the question text tells us that
the 2000 population increased by 1.8% to become the
2005 population of 21635 therefore to revert back to
the 2000 population we need to divide by (1+1.8%) or
1.018. If the question had said that the 2000
population was 1.8% less than the 2005 population
then we would have had to multiply by (1-1.8%) or
0.982 which is subtly different. When the percentage
is change is small the difference between the 2
answers is very small but make sure you understand
which to use when the percentages are larger.

working days were lost per 1 000 inhabitants between 1996 and 2005 on a yearly average, excluding 2001 and 2004?



139

141

154

173  ##Bonne réponse##

Explanation

Data Interpretation

From the table provided, we will be using the data
values referring to the number of working days lost
due to strike (per 1000 inhabitants, yearly average,
1996-2005) in Iceland. The rest of the table's
contents are irrelevant to our question. From the
question text we will be using the information that
there were 1571 working days (per 1000 inhabitants)
lost in 2001 and 1052 working days (per 1000
inhabitants) lost in 2004.

Reasoning

To calculate how many working days (per 1000
inhabitants) were lost between 1996 and 2005 on a
yearly average excluding the years 2001 and 2004 we
must first calculate the total number of working days
lost between 1996 and 2005. We then need to
subtract the number of days lost in 2001 and 2004.
Then with this new total figure we can divide it by the
number of years passed (excluding 2001 and 2004) to
find the new average.

Calculation

Firstly we calculate the total number of working days
lost:

401 days/year x 10 years = 4010 days (per 1000
inhabitants)

Now we can subtract the days lost in 2001 and 2004
from this total:

4010- 1571 - 1052 = 1387 days (per 1000
inhabitants)

To calculate the new yearly average we must divide
this by the number of years passed:

10- 2 = 8 years

1387/8 = 173.375

As we can see with rounding this means this is the
correct answer of 173 days per year.

Potential Shortcuts / Pitfalls

This question is relatively straight forward but can
sound confusing as a lot of information is given in the
question text. A good technique to use is firstly to
separate the actual question from the rest of the text
and then analyse the extra information given. Also
when calculating an answer like this, which involves
excluding data, remember not only to subtract the
number of days lost but also to subtract the years
from the amount of years passed.
